=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Anticipatory grief and bereavement adjustment

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Adult child (age not specified), recently lost mother
Clinical Goal: Assist the client in processing their grief, developing coping strategies, and improving self-care to regain a sense of control and well-being.
Summary: I'm still trying to adjust to life without my mother, who passed away last year, and I feel lost and disconnected from myself, struggling to find the motivation to take care of even my basic needs.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Sadness, Guilt, Disconnection
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing (e.g., "I'll never be able to move on"), Flooding (e.g., overwhelming emotions)
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening, Validation, Reframing (normalizing the client's experience), Empowerment (offering choices and autonomy in coping strategies)

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been thinking a lot about my grief. Losing my mother last year has left me feeling lost and disconnected from my identity. I can't seem to find the motivation to care for myself, and it's been a struggle to accept that she's truly gone.
