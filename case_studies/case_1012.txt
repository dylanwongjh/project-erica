Topic: Caregiver Burnout

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Primary caregiver, likely spouse, of a patient in a skilled nursing facility for six years.
Clinical Goal: To acknowledge and validate the caregiver's experience of sleep disturbances due to frequent nighttime calls from the facility staff.
Summary: A long-term caregiver is struggling with disrupted sleep patterns due to constant nighttime updates from her husband's care facility, feeling overwhelmed by the demands of her role.

Primary Emotions: Frustration, Helplessness, Fatigue
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing (assuming the worst-case scenario), Rumination (dwelling on the negative aspects)
Underlying Need: Emotional validation and support to manage caregiving responsibilities

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening, Validation, Normalization (acknowledging that her experience is common among caregivers)

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been the primary contact person for my husband [Name], who's been in a skilled nursing facility for six years now. The staff there calls me frequently, especially at night, to update me on his condition. Although I understand the necessity of these calls, they often disrupt my sleep.
